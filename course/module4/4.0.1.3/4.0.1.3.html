Physical Layer Protocols

Getting It Connected

Our home devices connect to the LAN using either the home router’s Ethernet switch or wireless access point. As mentioned previously, the router’s primary purpose is to interconnect networks. In this case the home router is used to connect our LAN to the ISP’s network giving us access to the Internet.

Figure 1 shows how the home router is connected to the Internet. Depending upon the type of Internet service, the home router may be connected to either a DSL or cable modem. The DSL or cable modem is then connected to your ISP’s network. The modem is usually considered the demarcation point between what is the ISP’s and the home user’s responsibility.

Some home routers may contain the DSL or cable modem embedded within the router’s Internet port. This is shown in Figure 2. In these cases a separate modem is not required.
