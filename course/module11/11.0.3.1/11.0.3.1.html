Growing to Larger Networks

Growth is a likely eventuality for successful small businesses, and the networks must grow accordingly, to meet the expanded needs of the bigger business. A network administrator for a small network will either work reactively or proactively, depending on the leaders of the company. Ideally, the network administrator has enough lead time to make intelligent decisions about growing the network to keep in-line with the growth of the company.

To scale a network, several elements are required:

  • Network documentation - physical and logical topology

  • Device inventory - list of devices that use or comprise the network

  • Budget - itemized IT budget, including fiscal year equipment purchasing budget

  • Traffic Analysis - protocols, applications, and services and their respective traffic requirements should be documented

These elements are used to inform the decision-making that accompanies the scaling of a small network.
